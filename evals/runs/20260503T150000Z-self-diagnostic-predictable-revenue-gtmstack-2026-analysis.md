# Predictable Revenue Diagnostic — gtmstack (Q2 2026)

## ICP Diagnosis

**The honest call: gtmstack does not yet have an ICP. It has four hypothesized
target profiles, none validated by a paying customer or by an external operator
running gtmstack on a live problem. Stating otherwise would be inventing
firmographics that the inputs explicitly disavow.**

The case file lists four hypothetical target profiles: GTM leads at Series B/C
SaaS or AI dev tools companies "in the chasm," founder-CEOs running their own
GTM at <50 employees, growth/strategy consultants, and portfolio-GTM operators
at VC firms. The inputs are explicit that "none of these profiles has been
validated by a paying customer or by an operator running gtmstack on a live
problem they faced." That is not an ICP. That is a beachhead question.

What an ICP at the level Cold Calling 2.0 needs would look like, if the
diagnostic infrastructure existed:

- **Firmographic**: Series B/C SaaS or AI-tools company, 50-300 employees,
  $5M-$50M ARR. (Hypothesis only.)
- **Behavioral**: GTM-leadership turnover in last 6 months, OR a public "we
  are repositioning" signal (Substack, podcast, board-deck leak), OR shipped
  a bottoms-up product that needs a top-down complement. (Hypothesis only.)
- **Buyer role**: Head of GTM / VP Marketing / Founder-CEO running their own
  GTM. (Hypothesis only.)

**Required: specific observable trigger event.** Until 5-10 paying customers
exist, the trigger event is itself a hypothesis to test, not a fact to act on.
The hypothesis worth testing first: **"public announcement of a new
Head-of-GTM hire at a Series B/C SaaS or AI-tools company within the prior 90
days."** This is observable (LinkedIn change-of-job notification, press
release, company blog), measurable (90-day window, a name and a title), and
hypothesis-relevant — a new GTM leader inheriting a stuck-in-the-chasm motion
is the case the tool's positioning targets. **This trigger is unvalidated.**
The first 5-10 paying customers, when they exist, will either confirm it,
correct it (different trigger), or invalidate the segment entirely (the buyers
turn out to be advisors, not operators; or boutique consultancies, not
in-house GTM leads).

The diagnostic question that resolves the ICP: **of the next 10 external
operators who run gtmstack on a live GTM problem (not a showcase case), what
job title do they hold, what company stage are they at, and what triggered
their interest in the tool that week?** That answer, gathered honestly via
Mom-Test-style customer development, IS the ICP. Inventing it earlier is a
Predictable Revenue failure mode (spray-and-pray outbound on an unvalidated
list).

## Role Structure

**The author is doing all sales. There is no sales team. There is no SDR.
There is no AE. There is no AM. The author writes LinkedIn posts, drives
modest engagement, hopes some of that converts. This is the founder-doing-
sales failure mode in its purest form, with one twist: there is no sales
motion at all.**

Mapping to Ross's three roles:

- **SDR (prospecting)**: zero. No outbound has occurred. LinkedIn posts are
  inbound-style awareness, not prospecting. The friend at the Series B AI
  startup who said "I'd run this next week" was Seeds-style (referral-shaped,
  warm), and even that didn't convert.
- **AE (closing)**: not applicable — there is nothing to close. Open-source
  + free + no pricing page + no commercial offering = no closeable unit.
- **AM (post-sale farming)**: not applicable — there are no customers.

The author has 10-15 hrs/week part-time. The author is not a full-time
founder. The author has not committed to making gtmstack a company.
Predictable Revenue's role specialization presumes a company building a
sales machine. gtmstack is presently a high-quality open-source project,
not a company, and the role-structure question is therefore premature in
its full form. The Ross-aligned diagnosis is: **before any role question
matters, the author must decide whether gtmstack is a company-to-be or a
side project. That is the load-bearing decision upstream of every other
GTM choice.**

## Lead Source Mix

Mapping the inputs to Seeds / Nets / Spears:

- **Seeds (referrals, word-of-mouth)**: present but inert. Two-three
  anecdotal "I'd run this" mentions from friends, one "this is the kind of
  thing we'd pay for," one "the convergent diagnoses match how we'd think
  about it." Zero of these have advanced past polite interest. Friends
  read the deliverables; nobody has run gtmstack on their own live GTM
  problem. **This is Seeds in the technical sense (warm, referral-shaped)
  but Seeds that don't convert are not pipeline; they are signal that the
  ask is too high or the value is unclear.**
- **Nets (inbound marketing)**: minimal. Public GitHub repo, README
  walkthrough, occasional LinkedIn posts drawing low-hundreds of
  impressions and single-digit reactions. No SEO, no paid ads, no events,
  no content engine. Star count and clone count exist but have not been
  treated as evidence — the instrumentation gap is itself the problem.
  Modest top-of-funnel signal, no measured conversion, no working funnel.
- **Spears (outbound prospecting)**: zero. No outbound has been attempted.
  No ICP list exists to target. No SDR exists to send.

**Dominant source today**: technically Nets (LinkedIn posts + GitHub repo
discovery), but at a volume so low it's noise rather than pipeline. The
"this is cool" replies are the closest thing to Seeds and the highest-
converting source on the table, but they are not converting either —
they're stalling at the "polite interest" rung of the commitment ladder.

**Neglected**: instrumentation. The author cannot tell whether Seeds or
Nets are working because there is no install count, no active-user count,
no end-to-end completion rate. **Lead-source blindness is present —
the author cannot answer "where is the most pipeline coming from"
because no source produces pipeline.**

**Mismatch with stage**: prescribing Spears now would compound the
problem. The author lacks (a) a validated ICP, (b) ACV high enough to
justify SDR economics, (c) capacity to specialize roles, and (d) a paying
customer base from which to extract the firmographic + behavioral pattern
Cold Calling 2.0 requires. The right Seeds-stage move is the one not
yet attempted: **convert one of the warm "I'd run this" mentions into a
live-problem session with explicit Mom-Test discipline (their problem,
not gtmstack's pitch) and watch what happens.** That is Seeds maturation,
not outbound.

## Outbound Recommendation

**DECLINE outbound. Defer Cold Calling 2.0 indefinitely. The conditions
under which Predictable Revenue prescribes Spears are not met, and several
of the book's named "when NOT to apply Spears" conditions are present
simultaneously.**

Mapping the inputs to Ross's named conditions for declining outbound:

1. **Pre-PMF.** No paying customers, no validated ICP, no demonstrated
   willingness to commit time/money/political capital. The author has zero
   evidence that the multi-book synthesis prescribes moves operators
   would *take* (vs. moves they would *nod at*). Until 5-10 external
   operators run gtmstack on live problems and convert to paid (or
   sustained) usage, the segment is unvalidated. Outbound at this stage
   would broadcast a pitch the company itself does not yet know is true.
2. **ICP not tight enough to write specific subject lines.** The author
   has four hypothetical profiles. Cold Calling 2.0 cannot generate
   reply-worthy subject lines from a four-way disjunction. "Hi {first} —
   noticed you're a GTM lead OR a founder OR a consultant OR a portfolio
   operator" is not a subject line; it's a confession.
3. **Seeds and Nets still have headroom unexplored.** The "I'd run this
   next week" friend has not been worked. No live-problem session has
   been booked. No Mom-Test-disciplined customer development has been
   done. Until Seeds is exhausted (and instrumentation tells you whether
   Nets has any signal at all), adding Spears is premature.
4. **ACV too low.** The product is open-source and free. gtmstack-
   specific monetization does not exist. There is no ACV to support SDR
   economics. (See CAC payback math below.)
5. **Author availability constraint.** 10-15 hrs/week part-time, no team,
   no funding. Even if the prior four conditions resolved, the author
   cannot operate the machine Predictable Revenue prescribes. Outbound
   that the author cannot follow up on burns the list and the brand.

**The conditions that must be true before outbound becomes the right
move:**

- 5-10 paying customers exist, with a discernible firmographic +
  behavioral pattern.
- An ACV is established at $5K+ per year (Ross's typical SDR-economics
  floor).
- A monetization motion exists (hosted version, certification, clinic-as-
  a-service — pick one and price it).
- The author has either committed full-time or hired a first commercial
  operator.
- A working Seeds + Nets motion produces top-of-funnel volume the author
  cannot keep up with.

Until that fifth bullet is true — Seeds saturating capacity — Spears is
not the lever. It is a distraction from the real lever, which is *getting
the first paying customer.*

**Required: counter-prescription circumstance.** The decline above flips
to a prescription if any of the following observable conditions hold:

- **Inbound saturates.** If installs cross ~500 in a 30-day window AND
  the author can identify a tight firmographic pattern in the actual
  user base, outbound to look-alikes becomes the next move regardless
  of the other conditions.
- **A paying customer cohort emerges.** If a hosted-version offering
  ships and 10+ companies pay $10K+/year ACV within 6 months, SDR
  economics become tractable and outbound becomes the right next move.
- **A specific trigger reliably converts.** If 3+ paying customers turn
  out to share the same trigger (e.g., "all of them hired a new VP
  Marketing in the prior 90 days"), the pattern is tight enough to
  outbound against and the decline inverts.

Absent these signals, the answer remains: **do not run outbound.**

## Conversion Math

The current conversion math is unmeasurable, which is itself the
diagnosis. The case is explicit: install count not measured, active-user
count not measured, end-to-end completion rate (skill-invoked → synthesis-
document-produced) not measured. Star count and clone count exist but
have not been treated as evidence. **The instrumentation gap is the
predictability gap.**

### Ratios that should exist (and don't)

- **GitHub-impression → install**: unknown. Should be measured; current
  signal is "low-hundreds of LinkedIn impressions per post, single-digit
  reactions, occasional 'I'd love to try this' replies that don't
  convert." Reasonable target band for an open-source dev-tool LinkedIn
  funnel: 0.5%-2% of engaged-impression to install.
- **Install → active use** (skill invoked end-to-end at least once):
  unknown. Reasonable target band for a CLI dev tool: 20-40% of installs
  produce one full session.
- **Active use → repeated use** (3+ sessions over 30 days): unknown.
  Reasonable target band: 10-20% of first-time users.
- **Active use → paid** (when a paid offering exists, which it doesn't
  yet): unknown. Reasonable target band for early dev-tool monetization:
  2-5% of monthly active users.

### CAC payback calculation (required v1 strict)

The author's time cost dominates everything else here. **Author time at
10-15 hrs/week part-time, valued at a strategic-consulting-equivalent
rate of $300/hr, is $156K-$234K/year fully loaded.** That is the entire
"sales + marketing + product" budget today.

If the author switches into a commercial mode (still part-time):

- **AE economics floor (Ross's rough rule).** A fully-loaded AE costs
  ~$250K-$300K/year. To be CAC-positive within 6 months, an AE-led
  motion needs >12 closes/year at $50K ACV, or >30 closes/year at $20K
  ACV, or >60 closes/year at $10K ACV. **At $0 ACV (today's reality),
  no quota math works. AE-led motion is impossible.**
- **SDR economics floor.** A fully-loaded SDR costs ~$120K-$150K/year.
  To pay back at 6 months, an SDR-fed pipeline needs to source ~$300K of
  closed-won. At $5K ACV (the rough Spears floor), that's 60 closes
  sourced per year per SDR. **At $0 ACV, no SDR math works either.**
- **Author-led founder-sales economics.** The author's time IS the SDR
  + AE + AM today. Each hour spent on a sales motion is an hour not
  spent on product, content, or the dogfooding-as-customer-development
  loop. **The "minimum quota" the author should set: 1 paying customer
  in the next 90 days at $1K-$5K MRR, sourced from the existing Seeds.**
  That single data point unlocks ACV, validates a buyer profile,
  generates a reference, and resolves the "is this a company?" question.
  Below that bar by Q4 2026, gtmstack stays open-source side project
  and the GTM machine question becomes moot.

The CAC math, in plain terms: **at today's ACV of $0, every minute the
author spends building outbound capacity has infinite payback time. The
profitable motion is the one that produces the first $1 of revenue,
because that's the first finite number in the calculation.**

## Sequencing

Quarter-level granularity. Each step depends on the prior.

**Q3 2026 — Validate Seeds (the only stage worth running).**
- Identify 3-5 named warm contacts who said "I'd run this." Convert
  exactly one into a live-problem session — Mom-Test-disciplined, their
  problem, not gtmstack's pitch.
- Instrument the basics: install telemetry, active-user count, end-to-
  end completion rate. Without instrumentation, no later stage produces
  signal you can read.
- **Dependency**: Seeds maturation must precede everything else, because
  ICP, ACV, trigger event, and monetization candidate are all downstream
  of "what does the first paying customer actually look like."

**Q4 2026 — Decide is-it-a-company.**
- Based on the 5-10 live-problem sessions accumulated by end of Q3, make
  the binary call: commercialize (hosted, clinic-as-a-service, custom-
  book-authoring, or certification — pick one) OR maintain as a high-
  quality open-source side project.
- If commercialize: ship a paid offering with a price tag tested against
  one of the warm prospects.
- If side project: stop running diagnostic loops on monetization
  candidates and accept the structural answer.
- **Dependency**: cannot run before Q3 because the input data (live-
  problem sessions, instrumentation results, willingness-to-pay signals)
  doesn't exist before Q3.

**Q1 2027 — IF commercializing, define the ICP from the customer base
that exists.**
- Look at the first 5-10 paying customers (if they exist by now).
  Extract firmographic + behavioral + trigger pattern from the actual
  base, not from the four hypothetical profiles.
- Set ACV target based on what the first cohort actually paid.
- **Dependency**: cannot run before paying customers exist (Q4 deliverable).

**Q2 2027 — IF the customer base is tight enough to outbound against,
build the first outbound motion.**
- Hire (or contract) the first SDR. Write subject lines targeting the
  trigger event extracted in Q1.
- Track lead-to-opportunity, opportunity-to-close, AE quota math.
- **Dependency**: cannot run before ICP is tight enough to write specific
  subject lines.

The Ross-aligned point: **Q2 2027 is the EARLIEST that Cold Calling 2.0
becomes a question worth answering**, and that path is conditional on
three sequential gates being passed. Today's right move is Q3 2026's
first sentence: convert one warm "I'd run this" mention into a live-
problem session.

## Confidence and Open Questions

**ICP diagnosis confidence: 3/10.** The case explicitly notes the ICP is
not yet definable from inputs. The diagnosis above is "no validated ICP,
here are four hypotheses to test" rather than a confident specification.
Confidence is in the meta-level diagnosis (that ICP is undefined), not in
any one of the four hypothesized profiles.

**Outbound recommendation confidence: 9/10.** Multiple of Ross's named
"when NOT to apply Spears" conditions hold simultaneously (pre-PMF, ICP
not tight, ACV $0, founder time-constrained, Seeds unexhausted). The
prescription "decline outbound" is robust across reasonable input
revisions; only a dramatic change (e.g., a paying-customer cohort
materializing in 60 days) would invert it.

### Specific facts that, if known, would change the analysis:

1. **Of LinkedIn engagement, what fraction installs the skill, and of
   those who install, what fraction completes one end-to-end synthesis
   session within 14 days?** Currently unmeasured. If install rate is
   >2% of engaged impressions and end-to-end completion is >30%,
   Nets is working better than the inputs suggest and Q3's instrumentation
   step becomes a Nets-amplification step rather than a discovery step.

2. **Of the 2-3 anecdotal "I'd run this" friends, what do they say
   when asked the Mom-Test sequence: "tell me about the last time you
   got stuck on a GTM question; what did you do; what would have made
   that easier?"** Currently un-asked. Their answer either confirms or
   refutes the demand-signal hypothesis at the level of "do they actually
   have the problem gtmstack solves." If the answers are vague or the
   problem is hypothetical-future rather than concrete-recent, the
   demand-signal hypothesis is weaker than the case file presents.

3. **What is the willingness-to-pay range from the warm contacts when
   asked "what would you pay for a hosted version with team features?"
   in a Mom-Test-disciplined commitment-ladder conversation (not a
   pricing-page dropdown)?** Currently unknown. If WTP clusters at $0
   ("I'd use the open-source version"), the commercial path is closed
   and the right answer is "side project, ship more books." If WTP
   clusters at $50-200/seat/month, hosted SaaS is viable. If WTP
   clusters at $5K-25K/engagement, clinic-as-a-service is viable. The
   monetization candidate decision in Q4 hinges entirely on this signal.

## Where the books diverge (the strategic questions still open)

The five books substantively disagree on three questions. These are the genuine strategic decisions the author cannot resolve from the convergence alone.

- Crossing the Chasm: founder-CEOs at YC-network seed-Series-A B2B SaaS startups in the Claude Code community.
- Obviously Awesome: VPs of Marketing or Heads of GTM at Series B/C SaaS, US/CA/UK, with three blocking criteria (read the books, has Claude Code, has an active beachhead decision).
- *Reconciliation:* Sequenced, not contradictory. CTC is right about the *first* visionary segment to chase (lower friction, faster feedback, founder-led decision authority). OA is right about the *eventual beachhead* (the buyer who recognizes "books-as-evals" as solving their problem). The Q3 2026 conversation should target CTC's segment; the Q4 2026 cohort should test whether the segment actually exists or whether OA's segment is closer.

**2. How much non-author validation is enough to act?**
- Mom Test: one non-friend operator with a live-problem run + post-conversation behavior change.
- Play Bigger: 5 external operators with written debriefs by 2026-12-31, 3 confirming prescriptive value.
- Predictable Revenue: 3 paying customers sharing the same trigger event before outbound.
- Crossing the Chasm: 10-operator cohort with ≥6 completions in 30 days to flip the chasm-position diagnosis.
- *Reconciliation:* Different thresholds for different decisions. Mom Test threshold = "is the hypothesis worth keeping at all?" PB threshold = "fire the niche-strike?" PR threshold = "start outbound?" CTC threshold = "shift TALC-stage diagnosis?" These are sequenced gates, not competing standards.

**3. Should the author commercialize, and on what terms?**
- Play Bigger: Path B is defensible as a part-time effort, but a commercial Path B requires ≥30 hrs/week.
- Predictable Revenue: monetization design is irrelevant until ICP is tight and 3+ paying customers exist.
- Obviously Awesome: positioning supports a commercial offering, but pricing is the easiest decision and should not be made first.
- The Mom Test: the binary "willing to pay or not" probe (asked correctly) is the highest-leverage data the author can gather and is missing entirely.
- *Reconciliation:* Defer the question to 2026-12-31 decision gate. Today's information is insufficient. Today's right move is gathering the data that resolves it.

## The Q-by-Q plan

This is the sequenced workstream all five books prescribe. Each step names the book(s) that prescribe it.

**Q3 2026 — the Mom Test conversation [Mom Test, OA, PR, PB, CTC]**
- Identify the single most-warm non-friend operator from the existing LinkedIn / GitHub / direct-referral surface.
- Schedule a 45-minute live-run session. Walk into it with no pitch, no demo, no synthesis-as-deliverable. Ask Mom Test past-behavior questions about the operator's last GTM strategic decision.
- If they have a live GTM problem, run gtmstack with them on it. If they don't, end the conversation politely; that itself is data.
- Outcome to measure: do they act on at least two prescriptions in the synthesis within four weeks, OR refer two peers? Anything else is Level 0 politeness.

**Q3 2026 — instrumentation [CTC, PR, PB]**
- Wire up install count, active-user count, end-to-end completion rate (skill invoked → deliverable produced), and dropout-point detection.
- Without this, the Q4 cohort experiment has no measurement frame. CTC, PR, and PB all flag this as a critical-path missing piece.

**Q4 2026 — 10-operator flip cohort [CTC]**
- Invite 10 operators to run gtmstack on live problems within a 30-day window.
- Threshold: ≥6 completions with deliverable-produced and post-run-debrief = early visionary segment forming, advance to next gate. ≤2 completions = before-chasm diagnosis hardens, demand-signal hypothesis falsified, recommend pivot or end.
- 3-5 completions = ambiguous, gather more data before deciding.

**2026-12-31 — the decision gate [PB, PR, OA]**
- Has the 5-debriefed-operator-with-prescriptive-value-confirmation threshold been hit?
- If YES → author commits ≥30 hrs/week for ≥6 months → fire Path B strike Q1 2027 (hosted version, eval marketplace, custom-book-authoring service offered to validated buyer profile).
- If NO → side-project posture is the honest answer. No strike. Continue dogfooding for the author's own use; do not pretend a company exists.

**Q1 2027 — Path B strike (conditional) [PB, OA]**
- Only if the 2026-12-31 gate passed. Lightning strike: ship the hosted version with named-customer reference, named-pricing, named-buyer-profile. Founders' segment first, beachhead segment second, consultant segment third.

**Q2 2027 — earliest outbound (conditional) [PR]**
- Only if ICP is tight (3+ paying customers share a common trigger event) AND ACV >$10K. Cold Calling 2.0 motion via SDR. Until both gates pass, no outbound.
